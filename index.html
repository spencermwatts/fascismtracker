What is fascism?


The diversity of fascism is such that any definition soon encounters evidence that does not fit.
Definitions of fascism vary across time and borders. There exists no simple checklist, no threshold for differentiating a fascist state from a non-fascist state. Our understanding of fascism amounts to an I-know-it-when-I-see-it kind of thing.

The qualities listed in the left-most column above comprise a small sample of characteristics that one may use to define fascism. This list is by no means authoritative or comprehensive.
Why should I care?

Acknowledging fascist characteristics of a state is not an exercise in futility, despite our inability to clearly define the term. Identifying fascist characteristics of governmental structures empowers individuals with an increased awareness of potential dangers and risks to our personal liberty.
